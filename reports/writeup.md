Long-only S&P 500 momentum, 2003–2026: 20.5% annualized return net of 20 bps round-trip costs, Sharpe 1.11, 3.4% annual alpha over equal-weighted benchmark. The classical long-short specification is uneconomic in this sample due to survivorship bias attenuating the short leg.

Unlike the classical pattern documented in JT 2001, where the cumulative momentum spread rises to roughly +12% by month 12 before reverting to zero, our 2003–2026 sample produces an inverted U-shape: the spread is slightly negative during the standard 6-month holding period, reaches a trough of approximately −5.5% around event month 26, and recovers to positive territory only after month 38. We attribute this departure primarily to survivorship bias from our use of current S&P 500 constituents. In a survivor-only universe, the bottom decile consists of large-cap firms with temporarily depressed prices rather than firms in genuine distress. These firms mean-revert with greater force than their winning counterparts, producing the negative spread over the medium-term horizon. The eventual reversal at very long horizons suggests that long-run quality/persistence effects may dominate once the initial mean-reversion of survivor losers has played out.

I regress the monthly returns of the long-only momentum strategy (net of 20 bps round-trip costs) on the Fama-French three factors. The intercept is +7.49% annually with a t-statistic of 3.89, indicating significant outperformance not explained by market, size, or value exposures. Adding the academic momentum factor (UMD, from French's library) reduces the alpha to +5.98% annually with a t-statistic of 3.66 — economically smaller but still highly significant. The strategy loads positively on UMD (β = 0.38), but the modest loading reflects construction differences as this portfolio is long-only and equal-weighted, while UMD is long-short and value-weighted. The strategy thus captures the long leg of momentum plus a small-cap tilt (SMB β = 0.30) and retains residual alpha beyond all four factors. On the other hand, the long-short variant has a UMD loading of 1.00 (t = 12.5) and no significant residual alpha, which means that the long-short specification is equivalent to UMD itself.
